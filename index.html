McMaster Tinkercad Challenge

Welcome, Innovators! Your challenge is to build a cool, smart device using a virtual electronics lab. You will use Tinkercad Circuits to build and code your project from scratch.

How to Wire Your Components: A Maker's Guide

In these projects, you won't be given a wiring diagram to copy. Instead, you will become a digital detective! Your mission is to figure out how to connect the components yourself using the clues available in Tinkercad. This is how real engineering works!

lightbulbThe Big Three: Power, Ground, and Signal

Almost every component has three types of pins you need to know:

  • Power (VCC, 5V, 3.3V): This is the "food" for your component. It connects to the 5V or 3V pin on your Arduino or Micro:bit.
  • Ground (GND): This completes the electrical circuit. It ALWAYS connects to a GND pin on your controller. All components in a circuit must share the same ground!
  • Signal (S, SIG, IO, or a specific name): This is the "brain" connection. It's how the Arduino sends or receives data from the component. This pin connects to one of the numbered I/O (Input/Output) pins.

mouseReading the Pins in Tinkercad

Your best tool is your mouse! When you are in the Tinkercad editor, just hover your mouse over any pin on a component. A label will pop up telling you exactly what that pin is (e.g., "Ground", "Power", "Signal", "SDA").

differenceAnalog vs. Digital Pins

On the Arduino, you'll see "Digital" pins (like 2, 3, 4...) and "Analog In" pins (A0, A1, A2...).

  • Digital pins are for simple on/off signals (like a button or a standard LED).
  • Analog pins are for reading sensors that give a range of values (like a temperature or light sensor).

hubSpecial Case: I2C Communication

Some advanced components, like the I2C LCD screen, use a special two-wire communication protocol. Instead of many wires, they only need four:

  • GND and VCC for power.
  • SDA (Data): This connects to the Arduino's dedicated SDA pin (A4).
  • SCL (Clock): This connects to the Arduino's dedicated SCL pin (A5).

This makes wiring much cleaner. Just match the pins: SDA to SDA, SCL to SCL!

Project 1: The Automated Smart Greenhouse

ecoYour Mission: Digital Farmer

Create a fully automated greenhouse that provides the perfect environment for plants to grow. It will monitor temperature, soil moisture, and ambient light, displaying all the data on an I2C LCD screen. It will automatically water the plants when the soil is dry and turn on a special "grow light" when it's too dark.

publicReal-World Application:

This technology is used in "vertical farms" and advanced agricultural centers to grow food efficiently with less water, year-round, even in cities. You're building a miniature version of a high-tech farm!

buildTinkercad Components

  • 1x Arduino Uno
  • 1x LCD 16x2 I2C Display
  • 1x Temperature Sensor (TMP36)
  • 1x Soil Moisture Sensor
  • 1x Photoresistor (for light sensing)
  • 1x NeoPixel Ring (12 LEDs)
  • 1x DC Motor (to act as a water pump)
  • 1x 10k Ohm Resistor

electrical_servicesWiring Guide

Use your detective skills! Find the Power, Ground, and Signal pins for each component and connect them to the correct places on the Arduino.

  1. I2C LCD Display: This is a special component. Connect its GND and VCC pins to power. Connect the SDA pin to Arduino pin A4 and the SCL pin to Arduino pin A5.
  2. Sensors: Connect the Temperature, Moisture, and Photoresistor sensors to three different Analog In pins (A0, A1, A2). Remember to give them power and ground!
  3. NeoPixel Ring: This needs a Digital pin. Connect its 'IN' pin to pin 6.
  4. DC Motor "Pump": Connect to Digital pin 9 and a GND pin.

psychologyCodeblocks Logic

Your code will continuously check the environment and react to keep the plants happy.

On Start

  • Initialize the I2C LCD (make sure to set its address, usually 0x27).
  • Initialize the NeoPixel Ring.
  • Show a "Greenhouse Ready!" message on the LCD.

Forever Loop

  • Read All Sensors & Update Display: Read values from the three sensors and print them neatly on the LCD.
  • Watering Logic: If the moisture level is too low, turn on the DC motor on pin 9 for 2 seconds, then turn it off.
  • Grow Light Logic: If the light level is too low, set the NeoPixel ring to a purple color. Otherwise, turn the NeoPixels off.
  • Wait for 1 second before the loop repeats to keep the LCD readable.

Project 2: The Keypad Security Vault

securityYour Mission: Virtual Security Expert

Go beyond a simple alarm and build a sophisticated security vault. This system is armed and disarmed using a secret passcode on a 4x4 keypad. When armed, a servo motor locks the vault door. If a PIR sensor detects an intruder, a loud alarm will sound and a light will flash, but only the correct passcode can silence it!

publicReal-World Application:

This is how real-world safes, secure rooms, and alarm systems work. They require a specific code for access (authentication) and have sensors to detect unauthorized entry. You're learning the fundamentals of electronic security.

buildTinkercad Components

  • 1x Arduino Uno
  • 1x 4x4 Matrix Keypad
  • 1x PIR Motion Sensor
  • 1x Servo Motor
  • 1x RGB LED
  • 1x Piezo Buzzer

electrical_servicesWiring Guide

The keypad is the trickiest part of this circuit. The rest use the standard Power, Ground, and Signal connections.

  1. Keypad: This component has 8 signal pins. Connect them in order to a block of 8 digital pins on the Arduino (e.g., pins 9 down to 2).
  2. PIR Sensor: Connect its signal pin to an Analog pin like A0.
  3. Servo "Lock": Connect its signal wire to a Digital PWM pin (marked with a '~'), like pin 10.
  4. RGB LED & Buzzer: Connect each of their signal pins to different digital pins.

psychologyCodeblocks Logic

You'll need variables for your secret code, the code the user enters, and the system's state (e.g., isArmed).

On Start

  • Create variables: secretCode (e.g., "1234"), enteredCode (empty), and isArmed (false).
  • Set the RGB LED to green (disarmed) and unlock the servo.

Forever Loop

  • Get Keypress: Read the keypad. If a number is pressed, add it to enteredCode. If '*' is pressed, check if enteredCode matches secretCode. If it does, toggle the isArmed state. If not, play a fail sound. Clear the enteredCode after checking.
  • System State Logic:
    • If isArmed is true: Lock the servo, set the light to blue, and monitor the PIR sensor. If motion is detected, trigger a loud, flashing alarm that stays on.
    • Else (if isArmed is false): Unlock the servo, set the light to green, and make sure the alarm is off.

Project 3: The Avionic Control System

flightYour Mission: Flight Systems Engineer

Construct the electronic brain of a model airplane! Using the Micro:bit, you will build a flight control system that manages engine throttle, landing gear, navigation lights, and a crucial flight instrument: the artificial horizon. This system will react to your commands and the plane's orientation, just like a real aircraft.

publicReal-World Application:

You are building a "fly-by-wire" system. Modern jets don't have direct cables from the pilot's controls to the wings; they have computers that interpret commands and manage the plane's systems. The artificial horizon you'll create is the most important instrument in a real cockpit for flying in clouds or at night.

buildTinkercad Components

  • 1x Micro:bit
  • 1x DC Motor (for the propeller)
  • 1x Servo Motor (for the landing gear)
  • 1x Red LED & 1x Green LED (for navigation lights)
  • 2x White LEDs (for landing lights)
  • 1x Piezo Buzzer (for stall warnings)
  • 1x Button (for landing gear control)
  • Resistors for the LEDs (e.g., 220 Ohm)

electrical_servicesWiring Guide

The Micro:bit has fewer pins, so choose them wisely! Remember to provide power and ground to all external components.

  1. Motors: Connect the DC Motor (propeller) to pin P0 and the Servo Motor (landing gear) to pin P1.
  2. Lights: Connect the Green navigation light to pin P8 and the Red one to pin P12. Connect both White landing lights to pin P16.
  3. Inputs/Outputs: Connect the external Button to pin P2 and the Piezo Buzzer to pin P14.

psychologyCodeblocks Logic

This system uses a mix of event-driven blocks and a main loop for real-time feedback. You'll need variables for throttle (0-100) and isGearDown (true/false).

On Start

  • Set throttle to 0. Set isGearDown to true.
  • Turn on the Red and Green navigation lights. Retract the landing gear (servo to 0 degrees).

Events

  • On Button A Pressed: Decrease throttle by 10 (but not below 0).
  • On Button B Pressed: Increase throttle by 10 (but not over 100).
  • On External Button (P2) Pressed: Toggle the isGearDown variable.

Forever Loop

  • Artificial Horizon: Read the accelerometer's tilt and map it to a single blinking LED on the matrix. Keeping it in the center means you're flying level!
  • Engine Control: Map the throttle variable to control the propeller speed on pin P0.
  • Stall Warning: If the accelerometer detects a sharp tilt (e.g., "roll" angle > 45 degrees), play a loud warning beep.
  • Landing Gear System: If isGearDown is true, lower the servo and turn on the landing lights. If false, raise the servo and turn them off.

Project 4: The IR Remote-Controlled A/C Unit

airYour Mission: Comfort Engineer

Build a fully functional model of an air conditioning unit. Using a standard IR remote, you will be able to turn the unit on/off, change its mode (Cool or Fan), adjust the target temperature, and make the air vent swing back and forth. All the current settings will be displayed on an I2C LCD screen.

publicReal-World Application:

Every remote-controlled device you've ever used, from your TV to a garage door opener, works on these principles. You are learning how devices receive wireless commands and change their behavior, a key concept in all of consumer electronics.

buildTinkercad Components

  • 1x Arduino Uno
  • 1x IR Remote and IR Receiver
  • 1x LCD 16x2 I2C Display
  • 1x Temperature Sensor (TMP36)
  • 1x DC Motor (for the fan)
  • 1x Servo Motor (for the swinging vent)

electrical_servicesWiring Guide

Use your wiring knowledge to connect all the parts. Remember the special connections for the I2C LCD!

  1. I2C LCD Display: Connect GND/VCC to power, SDA to pin A4, and SCL to pin A5.
  2. IR Receiver: Connect its signal pin to a digital pin, for example, pin 11.
  3. Temperature Sensor: Connect its signal to an analog pin, like A0.
  4. Motors: Connect the DC Motor fan to pin 9 and the Servo vent to pin 10.

psychologyCodeblocks Logic

This complex project needs several variables: isACOn, targetTemp, mode (1 for Cool, 2 for Fan), and isSwinging.

On Start

  • Initialize the I2C LCD and set all variables to their default off-state (e.g., isACOn = false, targetTemp = 22).

Forever Loop

  • Check Remote: Check if an IR signal is received. Use a big "if/else if" block to check which button was pressed (Power, Temp+, Temp-, Mode, etc.) and change the corresponding variable.
  • A/C Logic: Check if isACOn is true.
    • If it is, read the current temperature and update the LCD with all the info (Mode, Target Temp, Room Temp).
    • Control the fan motor based on the mode and whether the room temp is above the target temp.
    • Control the servo vent based on the isSwinging variable.
  • If isACOn is false: Turn off all motors and clear the LCD or show an "OFF" message.
